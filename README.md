# Arvi-Notes-on-Physics---Part-1
My original physics theory draft.

Date: 2026-05-24

Author： Ravi Ke

First, I want to clarify that this is only a personal hypothesis about the nature of gravity, not an established scientific theory.

I do not currently have mathematical equations for it, because what I am discussing is not calculation, but possible mechanism. Modern physics already has extremely accurate equations. I am only attempting to imagine a different intuitive explanation behind them.

Before explaining the idea, I assume the universe follows three fundamental rules (meaning there is no known underlying reason for them — they simply are how reality appears to behave):

1. The universe contains a smallest possible unit of space and matter.

2. One smallest unit of space can only contain one “thing” in a moment.

3. The velocity of an occupying smallest particle determines the displacement distance of the smallest particles it displaces.

I call the smallest particles “Wuzions” (pronounced “woo-zee-ons”).

---

I have always found it difficult to intuitively understand what a “force” actually is.

For example, with wind, we know it is fundamentally moving air. But what is gravity itself?

We can observe its effects, yet we cannot directly observe what it physically “is.”

So I began imagining another possibility:

Perhaps the universe is filled with extremely tiny particles — Wuzions. They are far smaller than electrons. Individually they may be sparse, but their total quantity is enormous.

The Earth rotates. And rotating systems often create vortices, like whirlpools in water or spirals in air.

So my hypothesis is this:

The Earth’s rotation may create a kind of “Wuzion vortex” around it.

As Earth rotates, it continuously disturbs and ejects these particles outward, while surrounding Wuzions flow back in to fill the imbalance. This constant motion may create an overall downward pushing effect, which we perceive as gravity.

---

One possible objection is:

If this were true, why do denser objects weigh more? Shouldn’t only surface area matter?

My idea is that because Wuzions are extremely small, they could move through the gaps between atoms and particles. As a result, denser materials would provide more internal regions for interaction.

Additionally, after collisions, Wuzions may lose energy or momentum, causing the overall motion to develop a directional imbalance instead of remaining perfectly random.

---

There are several reasons why I started thinking this way.

First, modern discussions of gravity often focus mainly on mass. But I began wondering whether rotation might also play a role.

For example, white dwarfs and neutron stars are not only extremely dense, but often rotate very rapidly as well. This made me wonder whether rotational motion could strengthen these hypothetical “Wuzion flows.”

I even speculate that, on a microscopic scale, particle binding itself could partially result from vortex-like interactions.

Interestingly, modern physics has discovered that many particles possess a property called “spin.” I understand this is not the same thing as classical rotation, but it still inspired this line of thought.

---

I have also wondered why large celestial bodies are usually spherical.

My guess is that during long-term rotation, sharp or irregular structures experience greater disturbance, while spherical forms distribute interactions more evenly and remain more stable over time.

---

Of course, I fully understand that this hypothesis currently lacks mathematical support and experimental evidence.

At the moment, it is more of a conceptual or philosophical model than a scientific theory.

It may be completely wrong. But I still feel that humanity may not yet be finished asking the question:

What is force, really?
